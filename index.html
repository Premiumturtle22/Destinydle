How To Play

Type a name of any enemy type in Destiny, and once you submit, you will see the attributes of the enemy displayed. Each attribute will have a colour associated with it, indicating how close your guess is to the answer:
Green: The attribute is an exact match.
Orange: The attribute has atleast one matching property.
Red: The attribute has no matching properties.
Additionally, some attributes may display an ⬆️/⬇️ arrow, indicating if the answer is higher/lower than your guess.

Attributes

Enemy

The image of the enemy you guessed.

Race

The race of the combatant, ex: Cabal, Taken, Hive. This can be useful if you guess a combatant with the same race as the answer, but in specific scenarios can lead to a partial match.
EX: Psion (Cabal) will be a partial match with Taken Psion (Cabal, Taken)

Damage Range

The range of the combatants weapon(s), ex: Melee, Short, Medium, Long. This information is taken from destinypedia and represents the most common range(s) of the combatant.

Damage Burn

The element type of the combatants weapon(s), ex: Solar, Arc, Void. Similar to damage range, this represents the most common element(s) of the combatant, and may include grenades.

Precision Spots

The area on a combatant that classifies your shots as precision shots, ex: Head, Chest, Back, Shoulder. This represents each of different possible precision spots, EX: Tormentor will have precision spots of Shoulder and Chest. In some cases, there is a possiblity this is can be None.

Tier

The tier of a combatant represents the general strength of the combatant. Lower tier enemies will be weaker, but come in large numbers. Higher tier enemies will be stronger, but more rare to encounter. EX: Thrall would be tier 1, while Ogre would be tier 4. This is different from combatant types (Minor, Major, Ultra).
*Note: Dread tiers are classified as what I believe they would be, since there is no information out yet.

Origin

The origin of a combatant is the game which the combatant was first introduced, ex: Thrall will be 1 (Destiny 1), while Tormentor would be 2 (Destiny 2)
